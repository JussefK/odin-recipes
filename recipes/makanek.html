Makanek

Description

Makanek is a traditional Lebanese sausage dish, packed with spices and flavor.
It's often served as part of a mezze platter. Here's a simple recipe to make Makanek from scratch:

Ingredients

  • 500g ground lamb or beef (or a mix of both)
  • 2 teaspoons ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Pine nuts, toasted

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the ground meat with all the spices, garlic, pomegranate molasses, salt, and pepper.
  2. Knead the mixture until well combined and slightly sticky. This ensures the sausages hold together while cooking.
  3. Cover and refrigerate the mixture for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld (or overnight for deeper flavor).
  4. Wet your hands with a bit of water or oil to prevent sticking.
  5. Take small portions of the meat mixture and shape them into small, finger-sized sausages (about 5 cm long).
  6. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  7. Add the sausages in batches, ensuring they aren’t overcrowded.
  8. Cook for 4–5 minutes on each side, until browned and cooked through.
  9. Once all the sausages are cooked, squeeze the lemon juice over them in the skillet and toss gently to coat.
  10. Transfer the Makanek to a serving dish and garnish with parsley and toasted pine nuts, if desired.
  11. Serve hot with pita bread and a side of tahini sauce or pickled vegetables.
