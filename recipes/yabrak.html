Yabrak

Description

Yabrak (also known as stuffed grape leaves or dolma in some regions) is a delicious and flavorful dish popular in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines.
Here's a classic recipe for Yabrak:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup short-grain rice, rinsed and drained
  • 250g ground lamb or beef (optional for vegetarian version)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, finely chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried mint)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Â½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 jar (about 200g) grape leaves in brine, rinsed and drained
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock (or water)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the rice, ground meat (if using), onion, tomato, parsley, mint, tomato paste, allspice, cinnamon, salt, pepper, and olive oil until well combined.
  2. Rinse the grape leaves thoroughly to remove excess brine.
    Trim any thick stems from the leaves.
  3. Lay a grape leaf shiny side down on a flat surface.
    Place about 1 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon of filling (depending on the leaf size) near the base of the leaf.
    Fold in the sides and roll tightly into a small cylinder, like a cigar. Repeat with the remaining leaves and filling.
  4. Line the bottom of a large pot with tomato slices or a few unused grape leaves to prevent sticking.
    Arrange the stuffed grape leaves seam side down in tight layers.
  5. Pour the olive oil and lemon juice over the grape leaves.
    Add garlic slices and stock (or water) to cover the leaves.
    Place a plate or heatproof dish on top to keep the rolls from unraveling.
    Cover the pot and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer gently for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the rice is cooked.
  6. Let the yabrak cool slightly before transferring to a serving dish.
    Serve warm or at room temperature with a side of yogurt or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
